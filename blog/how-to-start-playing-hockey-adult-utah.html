How to Start Playing Hockey as an Adult in Utah

July 20, 2026 • 7 min read • By Utah Glizzies HC

You watched the Mammoth run their first playoff series, you've been to a Glizzies game, and now you're wondering the same thing thousands of other Utahns are Googling right now: can I actually learn to do that? Yes — and you don't need a single day of on-skates history to start. Here's the real, verified path from zero experience to your first shift, using programs that exist in Utah right now.

Step 1: Get Comfortable on Skates First

Before you touch a stick, get your legs under you. Every major Utah rink runs public skate sessions, and spending a few of them just moving, stopping, and turning will save you weeks of frustration once you're on a team. The Utah Mammoth Ice Center in Sandy, Salt Lake County's ice centers (including Acord Ice Center), and the Utah Olympic Oval all offer public skate you can drop into with rental skates if you don't own a pair yet.

Step 2: Take an Adult Learn-to-Play Class

This is the actual on-ramp, and Utah has more of them now than ever thanks to the hockey boom the Mammoth kicked off. These classes exist specifically for people who have never played:

  • Utah Mammoth Ice Center (Sandy) — runs adult leagues and clinics built for a welcoming, inclusive entry point.
  • Utah Olympic Oval — hosts the NHL-affiliated Learn to Play Hockey program, plus its own adult leagues once you graduate.
  • Salt Lake County ice centers — four Olympic-size sheets across three facilities running learn-to-skate and learn-to-play classes alongside public skate and drop-in stick-and-puck.

Every single person in an adult learn-to-play class started exactly where you are. Nobody in that room is judging your first wobble — they had one too.

Step 3: Get Fitted, Not Fancy

You don't need top-shelf gear to start. Most learn-to-play programs only require skates and a helmet, and many rinks rent both for your first sessions — so you can try hockey before spending a dollar on equipment. When you're ready to buy, get your feet properly measured at a rink or pro shop, since hockey skate sizing runs differently than street shoes. Our beer league equipment checklist for beginners breaks down exactly what to buy first and what can wait, and our guide to the best skates for adult rec players covers what actually matters when you're ready to buy your own pair.

Step 4: Find a True Beginner Division

Once you've built some comfort in classes, look specifically for a "true beginner" or D-division league rather than jumping into a mixed-skill game — that's where the drop-off happens for most new adult players. Our guides to joining beer league hockey in Utah and finding a beer league team in Utah walk through exactly how to land on a roster once you're ready, division and all.

What to Expect Your First Season

Most adults spend a season or two in learn-to-play classes before joining a league, and that timeline is completely normal — this isn't a race. Expect to fall, expect to be the slowest skater on the ice for a while, and expect it to click faster than you think once you stop overthinking your feet. The adult rec hockey community in Utah is growing so fast right now that nobody blinks at a true beginner showing up.

The Bottom Line

Skate first, take a real learn-to-play class, borrow or rent gear until you know you're in, and target a true beginner league when you're ready. Utah has never had more on-ramps into this sport than it does right now — the only thing missing is you showing up.
